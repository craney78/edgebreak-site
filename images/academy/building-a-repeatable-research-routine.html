MODULE 3 • LESSON 14

Building a Repeatable Research Routine

Estimated Time: 25 Minutes • Advanced Breakout Research

Throughout the Academy you've learned the individual building blocks of breakout research. The final step is combining those concepts into a structured routine that you can repeat consistently over time. Great research isn't about spending every waking hour watching markets—it's about following an organised process that helps you recognise developing opportunities while avoiding unnecessary noise.

What You'll Learn

  • Why routines improve research.
  • How to organise a daily workflow.
  • Weekly and monthly review habits.
  • Using every EdgeBreak tool together.
  • Building continuous improvement into your research.

Consistency Creates Confidence

Successful research is rarely built from one exceptional day. Instead, confidence develops through repetition.

Following the same research process every day helps remove emotion, improve decision-making and gradually build experience observing how markets behave.

Over time, recognising quality market structure becomes far more natural because you've studied hundreds of charts using the same framework.

[IMAGE – Daily Research Workflow]

Start With the Breakout Scanner

Begin each session by reviewing the latest breakout opportunities.

Rather than assuming every breakout deserves attention, use the scanner to identify stocks that warrant further investigation.

Compare today's breakouts with previous sessions and observe how market structure continues developing after the initial breakout.

[SCREENSHOT – Breakout Scanner]

Search the NASDAQ Scanner

After reviewing active breakouts, use the NASDAQ Scanner to search for developing opportunities that have not yet broken above resistance.

Apply filters that match your preferred research style and study how different market structures compare.

Focus on identifying organised charts rather than trying to analyse every stock available.

[SCREENSHOT – NASDAQ Scanner]

Apply the Smart Money Filter

Once you've identified potential research candidates, apply the Smart Money Filter to highlight stocks displaying additional accumulation characteristics.

This extra layer of analysis helps narrow your watchlist while encouraging a more disciplined research process.

Remember that Smart Money characteristics support research—they do not predict future performance.

[SCREENSHOT – Smart Money Filter]

Review Charts Carefully

Scanners identify opportunities, but charts tell the story.

Open each stock using TradingView and review:

  • Overall market structure.
  • Higher highs and higher lows.
  • Resistance levels.
  • Volume behaviour.
  • Accumulation characteristics.
  • Weekly and daily trend alignment.

This step transforms scanner results into structured market research.

[SCREENSHOT – TradingView Chart]

Update Your Watchlist

Save only your strongest research candidates to My Workspace.

Avoid creating watchlists containing hundreds of stocks. A smaller collection of well-researched companies is often much easier to monitor and review.

Add notes explaining why each company deserves continued attention.

[SCREENSHOT – My Workspace]

Weekly Reviews

Once each week, review your watchlist from beginning to end.

Ask yourself:

  • Has market structure improved?
  • Have higher lows continued developing?
  • Has resistance changed?
  • Has volume behaviour remained healthy?
  • Does this stock still deserve further research?

Removing weaker candidates allows your watchlist to remain focused on quality.

Monthly Reflection

Every month, spend time reviewing your overall research process rather than individual stocks.

Consider questions such as:

  • What have I learned?
  • Am I following my routine consistently?
  • Which market structures have I recognised more easily?
  • Where can I continue improving?

Continuous learning is one of the most valuable habits an investor can develop.

[IMAGE – Continuous Improvement Cycle]

Your EdgeBreak Research Routine

Bringing everything together, a typical EdgeBreak workflow may look like this:

  1. Review the Breakout Scanner.
  2. Search the NASDAQ Scanner.
  3. Apply the Smart Money Filter.
  4. Study the charts.
  5. Review multiple timeframes.
  6. Save quality opportunities to My Workspace.
  7. Review your watchlist weekly.
  8. Continue learning through the Academy.

This repeatable process encourages structured research, reduces emotional decision-making and helps investors continually improve their understanding of market behaviour.

Lesson Summary

A structured research routine is one of the greatest advantages an investor can develop. By combining the EdgeBreak scanners, Smart Money Filter, TradingView charts and My Workspace into one repeatable workflow, members can build consistency, improve research quality and continue developing their understanding of breakout market structure over time.

Key Takeaways

  • Consistency is more valuable than constantly searching for new opportunities.
  • Use every EdgeBreak tool together as one research workflow.
  • Review charts—not just scanner results.
  • Maintain an organised, high-quality watchlist.
  • Commit to continuous learning and improvement.

Practical Exercise

Create your own personal EdgeBreak research routine.

  • Write down the order you'll use each EdgeBreak tool.
  • Decide how often you'll review your watchlist.
  • Schedule one weekly review session.
  • Choose one lesson from the Academy to revisit each month.
  • Commit to following your routine consistently for the next four weeks.
