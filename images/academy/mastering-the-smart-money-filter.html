Mastering the Smart Money Filter

Estimated Time: 25 Minutes • Mastering EdgeBreak

The Smart Money Filter is designed to help members narrow their research by highlighting NASDAQ stocks displaying organised market characteristics commonly associated with accumulation. Rather than identifying actual institutional buying, the filter searches for combinations of technical characteristics that may deserve closer investigation.

What You'll Learn

  • What the Smart Money Filter does.
  • How it complements the NASDAQ Scanner.
  • Why accumulation matters.
  • How to interpret filtered results.
  • Building higher-quality research watchlists.

Why the Smart Money Filter Exists

Thousands of stocks may satisfy basic scanner filters, but only a smaller number display the organised characteristics many investors associate with developing accumulation.

The Smart Money Filter helps reduce market noise by highlighting stocks displaying combinations of these characteristics, allowing members to focus their research more efficiently.

It is important to remember that the filter is a research tool, not a prediction tool.

What Does "Smart Money" Mean?

Throughout EdgeBreak, the term Smart Money refers to organised market behaviour that may resemble periods of accumulation.

It does not identify actual institutional investors or confirm that institutions are buying a particular stock.

Instead, the filter searches for combinations of technical characteristics that frequently appear together before some breakout opportunities.

[IMAGE – Accumulation Diagram]

How the Filter Works

The Smart Money Filter analyses several market characteristics simultaneously.

These may include:

  • Market structure.
  • Resistance behaviour.
  • Higher lows.
  • Volume characteristics.
  • Price organisation.
  • Accumulation behaviour.

By combining multiple characteristics rather than relying on a single indicator, the filter helps members focus on higher-quality research candidates.

Combining the Filter with the NASDAQ Scanner

The Smart Money Filter works best when combined with the NASDAQ Scanner.

A typical workflow may involve:

  1. Run the NASDAQ Scanner.
  2. Apply your preferred research filters.
  3. Enable the Smart Money Filter.
  4. Review the remaining results.
  5. Open each chart for further analysis.

This approach allows members to progressively narrow the market into a manageable group of research candidates.

Review the Charts

Scanner results should always be followed by chart analysis.

Once a stock passes the Smart Money Filter, review:

  • Weekly market structure.
  • Daily trend.
  • Resistance touches.
  • Higher lows.
  • Volume behaviour.
  • Overall chart quality.

The filter helps organise research, but the chart provides the complete picture.

Saving Research Candidates

When a filtered stock displays strong overall market structure, save it to My Workspace for ongoing monitoring.

Rather than making immediate decisions, continue observing how the stock develops over time.

This allows your watchlist to evolve into a collection of carefully researched opportunities.

[SCREENSHOT – Save to My Workspace]

Common Mistakes

New members sometimes misunderstand the purpose of the Smart Money Filter.

Remember:

  • It does not identify actual institutional buying.
  • It does not predict future price movement.
  • It does not replace chart analysis.
  • It should always be combined with your own research.

Used correctly, the filter simply helps organise market research more efficiently.

A Typical Research Workflow

An effective Smart Money research routine may look like this:

  1. Open the NASDAQ Scanner.
  2. Apply your preferred filters.
  3. Enable the Smart Money Filter.
  4. Review the remaining stocks.
  5. Study each TradingView chart.
  6. Save your strongest research candidates.
  7. Continue monitoring them inside My Workspace.

Consistently following this workflow helps build a disciplined and repeatable research process.

Lesson Summary

The Smart Money Filter helps members organise their research by highlighting stocks displaying combinations of accumulation-related market characteristics. When combined with the NASDAQ Scanner, TradingView charts and My Workspace, it becomes a valuable tool for identifying structured research candidates worthy of further investigation.

Key Takeaways

  • The Smart Money Filter narrows your research.
  • It searches for organised market characteristics—not institutions.
  • Always combine filtered results with chart analysis.
  • Use the filter to improve research efficiency.
  • Continue monitoring candidates through My Workspace.

Practical Exercise

Open the NASDAQ Scanner and apply the Smart Money Filter.

  • Review the filtered results.
  • Open five charts.
  • Compare their market structure.
  • Identify higher lows and resistance touches.
  • Save your two strongest research candidates to My Workspace.

Research Reminder

The Smart Money Filter is designed to support independent market research by highlighting organised technical characteristics. It does not identify institutional investors, predict future market performance or provide financial advice. Always conduct your own research before making financial decisions.
