How EdgeBreak Works in Practice

A structured approach to trade selection, execution, and management using EdgeBreak.

EdgeBreak provides a structured trading framework built on breakout setups, market intelligence, and defined execution principles.

The EdgeBreak Market Intelligence Engine measures how breakout setups are actually performing across the market, providing a clear view of whether conditions support participation or require greater selectivity.

Strong environments tend to support follow-through, while weaker conditions increase the likelihood of failure.

The objective is not to eliminate risk, but to recognise when conditions are favourable — and to manage exposure when they are not.

How Trades Are Selected

Trades are not taken indiscriminately.

  • B+ setups in supportive conditions
  • A setups in stable to strong conditions
  • A+ setups across all environments

Lower-ranked setups are generally avoided in weaker conditions.

How Market Conditions Guide Decisions

All trade decisions are filtered through the EdgeBreak Market Intelligence Engine.

  • When to participate more actively
  • When to reduce exposure
  • When to remain patient

Trade Entry

  • Confirmed breakout from a defined level
  • Evidence of strength or early follow-through
  • Price holding above breakout levels
  • Alignment with current market conditions

The focus is on entering valid structure — not chasing moves.

Trade Management

Trade management adapts to market conditions.

  • Strong markets → allow trades to develop
  • Weak markets → reduce risk quickly

Trade Exit

  • Loss of structure
  • Lack of follow-through
  • Signs of weakness or reversal
  • Deterioration in overall market conditions

Exits are based on behaviour — not prediction.

How Trades Perform in Different Markets

Strong Conditions: sustained moves and follow-through

Mixed Conditions: selective performance

Weak Conditions: higher failure rates

Risk Management & Reality

Markets are dynamic and unpredictable.

This approach provides clarity during more challenging periods — allowing activity to be reduced, exposure to be controlled, and patience to be maintained while waiting for more supportive conditions to return.

Rather than forcing trades in unfavourable environments, the focus shifts to preservation and selectivity until conditions improve.
