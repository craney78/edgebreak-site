Introduction

Breakout trading focuses on identifying when price moves from consolidation into expansion. These moments often produce the largest and fastest market moves, driven by shifts in supply and demand.

Market Structure and Build-Up

Breakouts form through identifiable structural conditions that signal a shift in market balance.

  • Multiple resistance tests
  • Higher lows forming
  • Tightening price range (compression)

Volume Confirmation

Volume is critical in confirming breakout strength. A valid breakout typically shows low volume during consolidation followed by a sharp increase at the breakout point.

  • Low volume = consolidation
  • High volume = breakout confirmation

Price Action

A strong breakout is defined by a clean close above resistance, supported by momentum and minimal rejection.

  • Strong close above level
  • Wide range candle
  • Continuation after breakout

Execution

Traders should avoid anticipating breakouts and instead enter only after confirmation.

  • Enter on breakout close
  • Or enter on next candle continuation

False Breakouts (What to Avoid)

Not all breakouts succeed. Many fail quickly and trap traders who enter too early.

  • Weak breakout candles
  • Immediate rejection
  • Price falling back below resistance

Risk Management

Risk management ensures long-term survival in trading. Stops should be placed below key structural levels.

  • Small losses are essential
  • Protect capital at all times

Exit Strategy

Profits are realised through disciplined exits. Traders should follow structure and trend until weakness appears.

  • Exit on lower highs
  • Exit on moving average break
  • Use trailing stops
