Understanding Margin Loans

A margin loan allows investors to borrow money to purchase shares. Your own contribution is known as your equity, while the remaining amount is borrowed from the lender.

The Loan-to-Value Ratio (LVR) measures the percentage of your investment funded by the loan. Lower LVRs generally provide a greater buffer against market movements, while higher LVRs increase borrowing risk.

This calculator is provided as a simple educational tool to assist with understanding margin lending concepts. It should not be considered financial advice.
